Represents a number of rendering operations on a surface.

In graphics parlance, a "render target" usually means "a surface (texture or framebuffer) bound to the output of a shader". This trait has a slightly different meaning, in that it represents the operations on that surface before it's actually bound and rendered. So a RenderTarget is built by the RenderBackend by inserting tasks, and then shipped over to the Renderer where a device surface is resolved and the tasks are transformed into draw commands on that surface.

We express this as a trait to generalize over color and alpha surfaces. a given RenderTask will draw to one or the other, depending on its type and sometimes on its parameters. See RenderTask::target_kind.
